Featured Projects

Asteroid Arcade

I made an android/browser game in Java using the LibGDX framework, writing the code and drawing the graphics myself. The player controls a spaceship with the keyboard or tilt controls and tries to survive as long as possible against waves of obstacles and the occassional mini-boss fight. They can collect coins to purchase new ships to play as.

Outer Wilds mods

I have made a number of mods for my favourite game, Outer Wilds, using Unity AssetBundles and C# scripts. The most popular of these (2000+ downloads) is New Horizons, a planet creation tool. No coding knowledge is required to create new planets for the game, the creator must simply create texture and heightmap files for their planet and then write a JSON config file which tells my mod how to set-up the new planet. I maintain documentation in the project's readme as well as a JSON schema to validate configs for users.

Other mods I have worked on can be found on the Outer Wilds Mods website, including a third person camera mod and "Half-Life Overhaul". The latter replaces most textures, sound, and models with those from the original Half-Life. My contribution was on importing the models from the GoldSrc engine into Unity, and making their armatures compatible with the animated skeletons in Outer Wilds. All my mods are open-source so that other members of the modding community can contribute to or learn from them.

Game Jam Projects

Here are just a few of the game jam games I've made. For each of them I did all the programming, music, and art with the exception of my platformer Apes Together Strong, where I collaborated with a friend who made the main menu and end screen backgrounds, and the character sprites.

Mockbuster Video

The theme of this jam was "rewind". The player has to sort VHS returns and sales while also keeping track of late fees and damaged tapes, and being sure that all tapes have been properly rewound after watching. Ranked 81st overall and got 46th place in game design out of 1,812 entries.

Apes Together Strong

The theme of this jam was "stronger together". The player can switch between controlling 3 separate characters in each level, each with different abilities needed to collect all the bananas needed to open the doors to the next level. Ranked 220th place overall and 83rd place in game design out of 1,859 entries.

Elementary

The theme of this jam was "retro" with an added restriction that the game could not exceed a 96x96 pixel resolution. The player moves atoms around in a grid, and has to bind them together into the required molecules to progress to the next level. They must make use of different tiles with functions like teleporting atoms or rotating the molecules to achieve their goal. I wasn't able to submit to the jam in time due to midterms.

Demos

These are some small prototypes I've made in Godot

Waves and bouyancy

I simulated Trochoidal waves using vertex shaders to make the graphical effect. To simulate bouyancy, I calculate the wave height at predetermined points on any bouyant object and use the depth to determine the force to apply.

Portal gun

I recreated the portal gun from Portal using teleportation and camera trickery.

Voxel terrain

I made voxel terrain from scratch, and used it to create different procedurally generated environments. I populated one of these environments with flying creatures that flock using the Boids algorithm, and flap their wings using vertex shaders.

PyTorch in Godot

I used PythonScript to incorporate PyTorch into Godot, and used it to create and train a neural network in playing a simple game of snake.

Procedural planets

I created 3D icosphere meshes and used random noise to set their terrain structure, then added gravitational forces to put them in orbit.

Conway's Game of Life

I made Conway's Game of Life with vertical and horizontal wrapping. Then I wrote a raymarching shader to project it onto a torus.

About me

My interests are in video games and astrophysics. I especially enjoy applying physics to video games, and I'm also interested in procedural generation and AI.

Outside of game development I enjoy participating in hackathons. My entries can be found on Devpost. My team won 1st place in the ImplementAI 2018 competition for implementing a Deep Q Network using RLlib to train an autonomous reinforcement learning agent to land a virtual spacecraft in 2D. In the 2019 McGill CodeJam competition my team won third place for using the semantic scholar API, plot.ly dash, cytoscape, and tensorflow to sort papers found on ArXiv by taking semantic embeddings of key-words and compressing them into 3D-space using principal component analysis.

I am also co-author on a published paper on the Primordial Radius Gap and Potentially Broad Core Mass Distributions of Super-Earths and Sub-Neptunes.
